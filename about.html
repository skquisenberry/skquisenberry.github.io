The App

This project was created for HackVT 2016 by Owen Marshall, Riley Wilbur, and Scott Quisenberry from the University of Vermont. The goal of the project is to create an intuitive, easy to to use, solution for locating community-based educational resources. With ACE, anyone can find educational programs near them.

ACE uses the Vermont.gov education datasets on the geography of public libraries and child care programs. It uses mysql, php, javascript, and the Google Maps API to process and display location data and contact information. While we came into this hackathon with a lack of experience in some of these techinical areas, we were able to learn quickly to complete the application.

The Cause

In numerous studies, early childhood education has been shown to greatly improve the likelyhood of academic success and high lifetime earnings, as well as reducing the risk of disipline problems and violent crime later in life (NIEER, High Scope Perry Preschool Study).

The ACE tool allows parents to more easily find early education services for their children, as well as access to other community education services in libraries and maker spaces which promote literacy and an interest in STEM. We hope that ACE could be used by families to find services they otherwise might not in order to attain greater personal success.
